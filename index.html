What is Rotaract?

Rotaract club is an organization for providing welfare services for the needful, organizing event management with co-ordination and developing an influential society with leading youngsters of leadership and workplace competency. The Rotaract Club involves the youth aged 18 and above, who have common objectives, motivation and enthusiasm to do various dynamic activities within the club promoting good relations, connections and unity among everyone both nationally and internationally.

Objective

The name “Rotaract” is a blend of the words “Rotary” and “action.” The unofficial Rotaract motto is Fellowship Through Service. Interota is the only Board-recognized triennial Rotaract conference.

Purpose

Rotaract clubs bring together people ages 18 and older to exchange ideas with leaders in the community, develop leadership and professional skills, and have fun through service.
